Northwind Labs — Team Handbook (2025 edition)

About Northwind Labs
Northwind Labs is a remote-first product studio founded in 2019, headquartered in
Lisbon with team members across 14 countries. We build data tools for the
renewable-energy industry. The company is organised into four squads: Platform,
Insights, Growth, and Reliability. Each squad is fully cross-functional and owns
its work end to end.

Working hours and remote work
We do not track hours. Team members are trusted to manage their own time and are
expected to overlap at least four hours per day with the CET timezone so that
squads can collaborate. There is no return-to-office requirement — Northwind is
permanently remote. Anyone may work from a co-working space and expense up to 200
euros per month toward it.

Time off
Every full-time employee receives 28 days of paid vacation per year, in addition
to their local public holidays. Vacation does not roll over past March 31 of the
following year. There is a separate, uncapped sick-leave policy: if you are unwell,
you rest, and no doctor's note is required for absences shorter than five days.
New parents receive 16 weeks of fully paid parental leave, which can be taken any
time within the first year after a child arrives.

Equipment and expenses
New joiners get a 1,500-euro budget to set up their home office, plus a company
laptop. Software subscriptions needed for your work are reimbursed without prior
approval up to 40 euros per month; anything above that needs a quick sign-off from
your squad lead. Expenses are submitted through the Fern portal and are paid out on
the 25th of each month.

Security basics
Two-factor authentication is mandatory on every work account. Passwords are managed
exclusively through the company password manager; sharing passwords over chat or
email is prohibited. Company laptops must have full-disk encryption enabled, which
IT verifies during onboarding. If a device is lost or stolen, report it to the
Reliability squad within 24 hours so it can be remotely wiped.

How we make decisions
Northwind uses a lightweight written-proposal process called a "Brief." Anyone can
write a Brief to propose a change. A Brief needs one sponsor from the affected
squad and a 48-hour comment window before it can be adopted. Decisions are
reversible by default; we prefer to try things and learn rather than debate at
length. Major, hard-to-reverse decisions instead require sign-off from two squad
leads and the founder.

Career growth
Every team member has a growth budget of 1,000 euros per year for courses,
conferences, or books. Formal performance reviews happen twice a year, in June and
December. Promotions are not tied to the review calendar — a squad lead can propose
a promotion at any time through a Brief.
